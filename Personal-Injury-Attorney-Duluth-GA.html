Determining Negligence and Liability in a Serious Injury in Georgia 

In a serious injury case, there is often one individual, or even several individuals, who are liable for the accident and had they made different decisions, they could have avoided the accident entirely. In most instances, an attorney is best suited to determine which individual(s) and entities have contributed to risk, negligence, and liability in a serious injury case.

The legal proof point, as it relates to serious injuries, considers something that is known as the “reasonable person standard.” This standard evaluates the way in which most reasonable individuals would be likely to behave in a particular situation under similar or the same circumstances. As citizens, we operate under a standard of care knowing that our behaviors impact those around us. 

This action and standard are known legally as “due care.” One example that many people can relate to is abiding by traffic laws and speed limits. While most people take due care and abide by all posted speed limits, individuals who do not follow these laws are not taking due care. Should they cause an accident as a result of their speeding and someone is seriously injured in that automotive accident, they have violated the reasonable person standard and are typically held liable in the eyes of the law.  If you or someone you love has been injured in an accident that was a result of the careless, reckless, or negligent actions of another person or business, you may be entitled to compensation for your injuries and losses.
